## Part 2: Job description

### Organizational context

This position resides within an economic analysis, policy, research, or program advisory function in a Government of Canada organization. The specific department, branch, directorate, regional or national scope, reporting relationship, and functional authority are to be confirmed.

The position supports evidence-informed public administration by conducting economic and socio-economic research and analysis, developing analytical products, and providing advice to managers and senior analysts. The position is not described as having delegated financial authority, direct supervisory responsibility, regulatory decision-making authority, or final policy approval authority. Any such authority must be confirmed by management before the job description is finalized.

The position applies economic concepts, quantitative and qualitative analysis, data interpretation, and knowledge of federal policy and program contexts to support the development, assessment, implementation, or monitoring of government priorities.

### Client service results

Provides economic analysis, research, briefing products, data interpretation, and advisory support to management, policy leads, program officials, and other internal clients so that economic evidence, risks, trends, distributional considerations, and policy implications are understood and considered in decisions, recommendations, and program or policy development.

### Key activities

- Monitors, collects, validates, and interprets economic, fiscal, market, labour, demographic, socio-economic, program, and other relevant data from internal and external sources.
- Conducts economic research and analysis to identify trends, risks, relationships, impacts, and policy or program implications.
- Develops analytical products, including briefing notes, reports, presentations, issue summaries, data tables, options analysis, and input to memoranda or decision documents.
- Applies established economic, statistical, and policy analysis methods to assess issues, compare options, and support recommendations.
- Contributes to forecasts, scenarios, costing assumptions, impact assessments, performance measurement, and evidence summaries, where applicable to the assigned work.
- Consults with internal colleagues and, as assigned, other federal organizations, provincial or territorial officials, stakeholders, or subject-matter specialists to obtain information and test analytical assumptions.
- Maintains documentation of sources, methods, limitations, assumptions, and analytical decisions to support transparency, reproducibility, and classification or program records.

### Skill

The work requires knowledge of economic theory and applied economic analysis, including concepts such as markets, incentives, costs and benefits, distributional impacts, fiscal or economic indicators, and uncertainty. The work also requires knowledge of public policy development, federal programs or mandates relevant to the assigned file, and the government decision-making environment.

The position requires skill in collecting, assessing, and synthesizing quantitative and qualitative information. This includes evaluating source reliability, reconciling inconsistent data, recognizing limitations, and presenting findings in a way that is accurate and usable by non-specialist audiences.

The work requires judgement to select appropriate analytical approaches, identify assumptions and risks, distinguish evidence from inference, and determine when issues require escalation to senior analysts or management. Direction is normally available through legislation, policy frameworks, program objectives, precedents, analytical standards, and supervisory review, but the position must adapt those sources to files where data may be incomplete or where economic effects are uncertain.

The position requires written and oral communication skills to prepare concise analysis, briefing material, and advice; explain methods and findings; respond to questions; and support collaborative work with colleagues and partners. The position may contribute to consultations or working groups but is not assumed to be the final organizational spokesperson unless that authority is confirmed.

The work requires skill using common analytical tools, data systems, spreadsheets, statistical or visualization software, and document preparation tools. Specific systems, programming languages, databases, or modelling platforms are to be confirmed.

### Effort

The work requires sustained concentration to review data, policy material, research reports, stakeholder submissions, and briefing products. Sensory effort is required when comparing datasets, checking calculations, reviewing documents, and detecting inconsistencies or errors in information.

The work involves managing multiple files, deadlines, and requests while maintaining accuracy and documenting assumptions. Periods of increased mental effort may occur when urgent briefings, time-sensitive analysis, or revised data must be incorporated quickly.

Physical effort is consistent with an office-based knowledge role. The work typically requires remaining stationary for periods of time while participating in meetings, reviewing documents, and using a computer. Any requirement for travel, field visits, extended on-site presence, or handling of physical materials is to be confirmed.

### Responsibility

The position is responsible for the quality, completeness, and timeliness of assigned economic analysis and related advice. It is responsible for documenting methods, assumptions, data sources, limitations, and review steps so that analytical products can be understood, verified, and updated.

Recommendations and analytical products may influence policy or program options, briefing advice, performance assessment, stakeholder engagement, resource planning, or management decisions. The position does not make final policy, program, regulatory, or funding decisions unless those authorities are confirmed separately.

The position is responsible for protecting sensitive information, respecting privacy, applying information management practices, and following applicable security, accessibility, official languages, and values and ethics requirements.

The position may coordinate tasks, contribute to project teams, guide students or junior staff on assigned analytical tasks, or review factual inputs from others. Direct supervision, budget management, staffing authority, and formal performance management responsibilities are not included unless confirmed by management.

Where analytical tools, algorithms, models, or AI-assisted methods are used, the position is responsible for maintaining human review, documenting assumptions and limitations, considering data quality and bias risks, and ensuring that outputs are not treated as final decisions without appropriate human oversight.

### Working conditions

The work is normally performed in an office, hybrid, or remote work environment using standard information technology equipment. The specific work arrangement is to be confirmed.

The work includes exposure to changing priorities, tight deadlines, requests for rapid analysis, and the need to reconcile incomplete or evolving information. The employee has some control over day-to-day analysis and work planning within assigned priorities, deadlines, and supervisory direction.

No unusual physical working conditions are assumed. Any travel, field work, exposure to distressing subject matter, shift work, overtime pattern, or operational response requirement is to be confirmed.
